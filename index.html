I am a Bioengineering MD PhD student at Stanford, interested in developing better treatments for severe psychiatric conditions, such as major depression and addiction. I am advised by Professor Karl Deisseroth and work with the Human Neural Circuitry program. I think part of the solution lies in better understanding how the brain and body give rise to these conditions, and targeting these proximal causes. To this end, I'm interested in systems neuroscience and reverse translation across species, computational psychiatry, and spatially and temporally targeted treatment approaches (such as neural interfaces).

I also think part of improving mental health broadly lies in improving the equitable access to fundamental rights like housing, healthcare, and economic opportunity. To that end, I'm a co-founder of the Stanford Housing Equity Project, a group organizing and advocating around issues of homelessness, substance use, and health equity.

I'm grateful to be funded by the Knight-Hennessy Fellowship. I previously received a BS in Physics at Yale, and have worked on systems neuroscience in small brains and closed-loop neuromodulation for treatment-resistant depression.

misc

  • I'm also really interested in critical psychiatry and the limitations of biological psychiatry and over-medicalizing human wellness. Better understanding the brain and building better treatments may offer part of the solution to helping people with psychiatric conditions, but it's just as urgent to address the social determinants of these conditions -- like inequality, trauma, and racism. Please reach out if you ever want to chat about this, I'm always trying to learn more!
